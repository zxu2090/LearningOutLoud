About

Zhen Xu徐真

Welcome! My name is Zhen Xu. I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. I earned my Ph.D. in Educational Technology from the University of Florida, with a minor in Quantitative Research Methods.

My work integrates instructional technology research, teaching, and service, with a strong emphasis on building pre-service and in-service teachers’ capacity to use artificial intelligence (AI) responsively and effectively and to teach data science across K–12 and higher education contexts. My current research advances two complementary areas: (1) the design of AI-powered teaching and learning tools and (2) strategies for building teachers capacity to teach with and about AI across K–12 contexts.

Methodologically, my work is grounded in community-engaged research. I prioritize the needs of teachers, students, families, and school districts, and I co-design solutions with community partners, positioning them as co-researchers in the knowledge-building process. I employ design-based research, quantitative methods, mixed methods, and learning analytics approaches to investigate how teachers and students interact with AI tools and how these interactions can be improved to support learning.
